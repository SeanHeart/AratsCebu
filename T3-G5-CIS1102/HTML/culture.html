Sinulog

Sinulog festival is one of the most famous festivals and biggest festivals in Cebu held every third Sunday of January to honor the baby Jesus, Santo Niño. This event is a colorful, vibrant, cacophonic but heart-beating and exciting celebration.It is celebrated for nine days that culminates on the final day with a Sinulog Grand Parade.

Usually, a Fluvial Procession is held at dawn using a motor boat loaded with hundreds of flowers, candles, and the Santo Niño on top before the grand parade. The Fluvial procession starts from Mandaue City until it reaches the Basilica Minore del Santo Niño, where a performance will be held which is a re-enactment of the island’s Christianization.

Wake or Vigil

https://www.facebook.com/medellin.cebu/posts/vigil-at-the-wake-of-cong-benhur-salimbangon-at-the-curva-covered-court-requiem-/1774248029391141/

Wake or vigil is one peculiar aspect of Cebu’s customs and traditions which is still in practice to this day. Wake usually takes five to seven days or even longer if the bereaved family is still waiting for someone’s arrival from distant provinces or from outside of the country. The dead body is preserved by embalming it and placing it in a coffin surrounded with some ornaments, including flowers, photo of the dead, and funeral lights.

During these five to seven (or longer) days, the visitors are given something to eat such as snacks. The family members or at least someone should stay awake during the night because they are not allowed to leave the dead alone, so what they do on this occasion is that people would either talk for the whole night or play board games, card games and some are even gambling to pass the time. But before the games and talking, there is a scheduled time to pray for the dead.

Superstitions

Superstitious beliefs do not only apply to Cebuanos but also the people outside of Cebu as well. Some of the superstitious beliefs for Cebuanos are, black cats are a sign of bad luck or death, sweeping the floor when there’s a wake, fishbone dilemma, and more.

Cats can be a symbol of luck and prosperity in other countries but in Cebu, a lot of people believe that a black cat is a sign of bad luck. Accidentally crossing paths with one is taken as a bad omen of misfortune and even death.

Cebuanos believe that when you sweep the floor during a wake it could drive away the spirit of the dead and may bring bad luck, such as another family member might follow the dead to the afterlife.

For the fishbone dilemma, when you have a fishbone stuck in your throat, Cebuanos believe that letting a cat gently scratch your throat will remove the fishbone. But when doing so, make sure that the cat is not aggressive as it can cause serious scratches around your neck.

Birth

https://images.app.goo.gl/X779bWTSbwqGR1EcA

  • The Cebuano family welcomes the coming of a child by being there for the person who is giving birth.
  • They believe that placing pomelo leaves under the house will drive away all the evil spirits that may have lurked near the surroundings.
  • Also, while the woman is in labor, the family will burn some dried leaves and roots so that the smoke will somehow guide or protect the woman from the painful labor.
  • Having a boy as one’s first child is believed to be good luck.
  • After the child is born, the placenta is buried on the seashore to ensure the health of the baby.
  • During the child’s first haircut, some pieces are placed inside a prayer book or any book because they believe it will make their child a wide reader when he/she grows up.

    Baptism

    After a child has been born it is the custom of the Cebuanos to baptize the child after a few days or few weeks. During that time godparents are carefully chosen because they are the child’s second parent/s. It is necessary to carefully choose the godparents of the child because the parents want their child to emulate the good examples shown by his/her godparents.

    The practice of baptizing a child isn’t only done to the people living in Cebu, but also to the other parts of the Philippines and other countries outside of the Philippines as well.

    Courtship

    https://images.app.goo.gl/ujHdMjcfwgCwhMtr9

    Similar to other places in the Philippines, Cebuanos also have this serenade or harana in Filipino, but in Cebuano, it’s called ‘balak’. This is when a man is in love with a girl. He takes his first step by visiting her at home with the consent of the girl’s parents, dressed in his best clothes, and bringing homemade delicacies and serenades. Sometimes the man will bring another person or a third person to act as his go-between to help him win over the girl he loves. Another important requirement when serenading is when the parents of the girl ask for a living dowry consisting of a certain amount of money and some valuable pieces of jewelry.

    When a man is doing a serenade to the girl he loves, it takes him a very long time before the girl answers his proposal.

    Marriage

    The preparation for the wedding party is done at the groom’s house and part of it is brought to the bride’s house which is to be served to her guests and relatives. The expenses for the party are the groom’s responsibility.

    After the wedding ceremony, the newlyweds would walk together towards the house of the bride. Then, there are two plates filled with water and some San Francisco leaves placed at the foot of the stairs for them to step on it. A family member of the bride, precisely an old woman, has to comb the hair of the newlyweds and offers some advice to live a happy life together.

    It is believed that they will have an orderly and harmonious life if they step on the plates and if the hair of the newlywed is combed.

    Funeral

    https://images.app.goo.gl/toa2zJmHPt2GuFSp7

    During the burial day of someone who died, it is part of Cebu’s customs to have a procession towards the church, have a mass there and another procession towards the cemetery and a prayer will be held. The coffin with the deceased is either carried by family members (or male relatives) or loaded into a hearse.The family and relatives of the deceased are usually wearing black colored clothing to symbolize their grief or sorrow.

    After the burial, there will be an evening prayer for nine days (also called as pasiyam). It is believed to be the day when the soul leaves the world of the living.

    Walk With Jesus

    Walk with Jesus marks the start of the yearly Sinulog festival celebration. Starting at four in the morning, thousands of devotees endure the cold to be in a solemn procession that starts at Fuente circle and ends at the Basilica. During the procession, devotees light their candles and speak of silent prayer or sing the song “Bato-Balani sa Gugma” in unison. When viewed from the top, it would look like a river of light flowing.

    Spanish Heritage

    https://images.app.goo.gl/usKf3BuMHAQG9Wt5A

    Cebu City retains its long Spanish heritage. There is a site where Ferdinand Magellan landed on April 7, 1521. He sealed a blood compact with the chief of Cebu, Humabon. But later on, he was killed by Chief Lapulapu of nearby Mactan Island.

    Cebu was the Spanish colonial capital for six years and it remained the primary Spanish bastion in the southern part of the country.
